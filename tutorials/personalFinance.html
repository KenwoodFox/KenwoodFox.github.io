A Personal Finance Guide for the Modern Person

By Joe Sedutto, September 2018

    Personal finance, a lot of people know right off what personal finance is, it's your personal, finances, your money, and more importantly, how you manage your own money. If you search "Personal Finance" into google, you get, thousands, millions, almost a billion results and counting articles and blogs and studies all with an underlying piece of advice "Save more than you Spend" well, there you go, personal finance seems easy, with such simple advice at our fingertips, it makes me wonder why in this year, the average person spends 133% of their income.

    133% that's an extra 1/3d dollar spend for each dollar they earn, people overspend, people go into debt, why? In a study 2017, with research from a year or so earlier, CNBC reported that;
"Only 8 percent of [people polled] had a high level of knowledge [of personal finance], while about a quarter, or 24 percent, demonstrated just a basic understanding of how to manage their money. Yet 69 percent gave themselves high marks for financial know-how."
-Jessica Dickler, "Millennials aren't as smart about money as they think"  
if personal finance is so easy how can you have millions of Americans going into debt like this. Obviously, we can figure this out, its clear this wisdom is old, unrefuted, and, by the 133% fact, the advice is ignored, so what can you do?

    People need another kind of article, a guide that does not just tell you what to do to not overspend, a guide that tells you why you are overspending, because its clear that using conventional wisdom is not working, just like telling a smoker that smoking is bad, telling a person that debt is bad won't make them stop.

    Social Pressure. Social pressure is just what it sounds like, society putting pressure on you, you experience social pressure when you're waiting in line for coffee, you don't cut in front because society tells you it's bad, you wear clothes when you're in town because society has pressured you to do that, and that's ok, social pressure is a double edged sword, able to keep a community together, but also able to make a community do something, for example, when a group or community decide that drugs and using drugs are important, they may start to apply a social pressure on those outside them, to convince these people to try what they are doing. As humans, we are creatures of social tendencies, we want to fit in somewhere, even if it's not the mainstream. So, what do social pressures have to do with finances? Advertising. Why do you buy what you buy? Why buy these expensive shoes over the plain cheaper ones? Advertising seeks to make you think something you want, like a fancy pair of shoes, is something you need, a purchase you need to fit into society, and that pressure is a great factor in making you pick a product, hire a service, or even vote for a political party.

  • Finance - Finance is money, the management and use of money when you finance a thing you send money, when you manage your finances you are controlling the flow of money that you have
  • Debt - Debt is when you owe something, you are in debt if you owe money, services, or something else to somebody else.
  • Invest - To invest is to put forth some wealth now and expect a growth later, for example, stocks, bonds. You can imagine it like a farmer watering his crops, putting in some work and wealth now for a harvest later that will be worth more than his original imput
  • Income - income is money coming in, whether it be salary, commision, or investments
  • Conventional wisdom - conventional wisdom is old wisdom, useful but, not in debt and not always applicable in places where its used (STUB)
  • Advertising - advertising is a form of media, a medium, advertising seeks to make it seems as though something that you want, like a beauty product, a car, a computer
  • Value: Depreciate - Loose value over time, the opposite of an investment, a car is an example, it loses value as time goes on
  • Liablity - A liability is something you owe or are owed but do not have, loans, mortgages, etc.
  • Credit - When you obtain goods or services before the payment is moved, ie. bar tab, credit card, its a temporary loan
  • Credit Score - a score or number assigned to a person that based on past events determines their capacity to pay back credit cards and loans, a good score may allow you to get a lower interest rate for a loan
